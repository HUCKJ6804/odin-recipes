Pupusas

The Food of the Gods

Description

Latino-El Salvador-Intermediate

You're here for the recipe and how to get this deliciousness onto a plate to be consumed as soon as possible, so let's go.

Ingredients

1 cup of Masa flour yeilds 4 pupusas.

  • Masa - 4 cups
  • Queso Fresco 1 16oz
  • Cooking oil -1 cup ( I use olive oil, but whatever one you prefer)
  • Salt - 4 teaspoons
  • Water - 4 1/2 cups warm water

Steps

  1. Take the Queso Fresco and crumble it with your hands in a medium sized bowl.

    Place into the fridgerator to remain chilled until needed later on.

  2. In a large bowl pour the 4 cups of Masa.
  3. Add the 4 teaspoons of Salt.
  4. With either you hand or a fork, stir the salt in with the Masa until the salt is throughly mixed.
  5. Take the 1 cup of Olive oiland add it to the Masa/salt mix.
  6. Mix the Olive Oil into the Masa with the fork or you hands.
  7. Take out a skillet and set the temperature around 350 degrees.
  8. As the skillet gets to temp, take the 4 1/2 cups of warm water and add it to the mix 1 cup at a time until all is in.
  9. Take the Queso Fresco from the fridgerator.
  10. Get your hands wet and grab a ping pong ball sized amount of Masa dough and roll it to resemble a ball.
  11. Place your thumbs into the ball and create a bowl from the dough. (This is where the Queso Fresco will be placed into).
  12. Take about a tablespoon of Queso Fresco and place it within the Masa bowl you've made.
  13. With your fingers pinch the bowl closed so no Queso Fresco comes out.
  14. Flatten the Masa and Queso with your hands.

    To avoid the Masa getting stuck to your hands and undoing all the work you've done, you want to alternate the hand that is pushing the ball flat.

  15. Once the ball resembles an engorged tortilla place it onto the skillet.
  16. After around 3 minutes flip the pupusa to the other side.
  17. Rinse and repeat until the Masa mix is gone, and you have a lovely stack of pupusas to be consumed by your lonesome, or with loved ones (if you want to share).
